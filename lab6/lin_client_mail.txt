Rocky Linux Email Client (Thunderbird) Setup
STEP 1 — Install Thunderbird (Rocky Linux)

On the Rocky Linux client, open a terminal:

sudo dnf install -y thunderbird


Launch it:

thunderbird &


(Lab requires installing and configuring an MUA on Rocky Linux) 

Lab06_Instructions (1)

STEP 2 — Open Account Settings

In Thunderbird:

Right-click Local Folders

Click Settings

STEP 3 — Add Mail Account

In Account Settings:

Click Account Actions

Click Add Mail Account…

STEP 4 — Enter User Info

Example values:

Your Name: Starlord

Email Address: starlord@maafa.com

Password: (mailbox password)

Click Continue 

Lab06_Instructions (1)

STEP 5 — Automatic Configuration (Preferred)

If DNS + MX are correct:

Thunderbird will show Configuration Found

Click Done

This confirms DNS/MX discovery is working (lab notes this) 

Lab06_Instructions (1)

STEP 6 — Manual Configuration (If Auto Fails)

Click Manual Configuration and set:

Incoming

Recommended for the lab:

Protocol: IMAP

Server: server.maafa.com

Port: 143

SSL: None

Authentication: Normal password

(POP3 is optional; IMAP is what the lab focuses on for traffic capture later) 

Lab06_Instructions (1)

Outgoing (SMTP)

Server: server.maafa.com

Port: 25

SSL: None

Authentication: Normal password

Click Done 

Lab06_Instructions (1)

STEP 7 — Security Warning (Expected)

Because TLS was not configured:

Check “I understand the risks”

Click Confirm

Click Finish

Lab says this warning is expected

STEP 8 — Verify Mailbox Loads

You should see:

Account listed on the left

Inbox loads

STEP 9 — Test Email Delivery

Send a test email:

To: starlord@maafa.com

Subject: Test Email

Confirm:

Email sends

Email arrives (in Inbox)

Lab requires sending/receiving verification screenshots

Troubleshooting (Linux Client Commands)

Run:

ping server.maafa.com

nslookup server.maafa.com

nslookup -type=mx maafa.com
